Project OverviewThis project involves a comprehensive diagnostic analysis of retail sales data. The objective is to identify growth drivers, seasonal purchasing trends, and regional profitability to provide actionable insights for inventory and marketing optimization.

 Core Functional Requirements MetData Cleaning & Preparation: Standardized date formats, handled null values in postal codes, and removed duplicate entries.Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA): Performed a deep dive into sales and profit distributions across 4 years of data.Seasonality & Trends: Identified peak sales months (November/December) and calculated Month-over-Month (MoM) growth.Product & Regional Analysis: Segmented performance by Category (Technology, Furniture, Office Supplies) and Region (West, East, Central, South).Profitability Assessment: Isolated "loss-making" sub-categories and regions to suggest cost-reduction strategies.

Strategic InsightsHoliday Season Spike: Analysis shows a consistent 30-40% increase in sales during Q4, driven primarily by the "Consumer" segment.Category Bottlenecks: While "Furniture" generates high sales, it often has the lowest profit margin due to high shipping costs and returns.Shipping Impact: "Standard Class" shipping is the most used, but "First Class" shows a higher correlation with high-value technology purchases.Discount Sensitivity: High discounts (above 20%) in the "Office Supplies" category significantly erode profit without a proportional increase in volume.🛠️ Technology StackPython 3.xPandas: For time-series resampling and data manipulation.Matplotlib & Seaborn: For trend lines, heatmaps, and category comparisons.Plotly (Optional): For interactive sales-by-state maps.

 Data-Driven RecommendationsInventory Stocking: Increase stock levels for Technology products starting in October to prepare for the Q4 surge.Discount Optimization: Reduce deep discounting in the "Furniture" category, focusing instead on bundled shipping to preserve margins.Regional Focus: Expand marketing efforts in the Central region, which shows the highest potential for growth in "Office Supplies."
