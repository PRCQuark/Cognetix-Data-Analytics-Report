Project Overview
This project performs an Advanced Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a selection of high-growth technology stocks and market-representative ETFs. The goal is to evaluate individual stock performance against broader market benchmarks to identify Alpha (market-beating returns) and assess risk-adjusted performance.

Key Features
Multi-Asset Comparison: Comparative analysis of Apple (AAPL), Microsoft (MSFT), and Alphabet (GOOGL).

Market Benchmarking: Performance tracking against the S&P 500 (SPY) and Nasdaq-100 (QQQ).

Technical Indicators: Implementation of 20-day and 50-day Simple Moving Averages (SMA) to identify trend reversals.

Risk Metrics: Calculation of Annualized Volatility and Correlation Matrices to evaluate portfolio diversification.

Visualizations & Insights
Cumulative Returns: Identified that Microsoft (MSFT) was the top performer, significantly outstripping the S&P 500 benchmark.

Volatility Analysis: Confirmed that while individual tech stocks offer higher returns, they carry ~1.5x the price volatility of the diversified SPY ETF.

Correlation Heatmap: Observed a high correlation (0.90+) between the selected stocks and QQQ, indicating strong sector-driven movement.

Trend Analysis: Used Moving Average crossovers to pinpoint historical entry and exit points for AAPL.

Technologies Used
Python 3.10+

Pandas: Data manipulation and cleaning.

Matplotlib/Seaborn: Static data visualization.

Plotly: Interactive dashboarding.
